VELA is a personalised weather forecast application, specifically designed
to help water-sport aficionados stay safe & plan a relaxing day on the water
…even if they lack the experience!

The project was accompanied by 10 months of user experience design
training, followed by 3 months of front-end development. This deepened my
knowledge in user experience design and allowed me to both explore and
cement my own design process.

Competitive Analysis

To begin understanding the user, I had to put myself in their shoes. I
conducted extensive research on the weather-related sports marketplace, to
observe which apps my target audience were currently using. The niche was
already well established with popular tools such as Windy and Windfinder
claiming most of the following.

I conducted my competitive analysis on these two apps including the key
objectives, market advantages, marketing profile, SWOT analysis & UX analysis. This
allowed me to understand the preferences & pain points associated with their use.

Bottom Line

Although there are well established weather applications on the market,
these tools are not meeting the needs of water-sport enthusiasts. VELA
can capitalise on these weaknesses by displaying complex weather data
in an accessible & easy-to-understand way for a specified target audience.

Surveys & Interviews

Equipped with a basic understanding of the market, I conducted my own user
research using the tools available to me. I conducted a survey to gather
quantitative data and build a better image of my target market. Participants
were gathered from Facebook groups, Slack channels & my personal network.

After interpreting data from the survey responses, it was time to take a more intimate
approach. In order to gain qualitative insights into the routines & behaviours of my
audience, I conducted user interviews. Participants were chosen from my personal
network based on their interest in water sports.

User Personas

Using both the quantitative & qualitative data from the research results, it was
time to bring the data to life! I created 2 user personas which allowed me to
personify my target audience & prioritise their goals according to their needs.

Information Architecture

With user journeys in hand, I could then dig into the steps necessary for
customers to complete these tasks with VELA. Upon analysing the data I was
able to structure the information & organise it into some basic sitemaps.

Having drafted the initial sitemaps, I conducted an open card sort. This was
composed of 30 cards, which 12 participants organised into groups. I gained a
further understanding of my user’s mental-model while iterating my sitemap.

Wireframing & Prototyping

Having empathised with my audience & generated ideas from the data, I could

User Testing

With a mountain of iteration behind me, I now had a high-fidelity prototype to
test on real users. The overall goal of these usability tests was to discover new
opportunities while learning more about the user’s common behaviours &
preferences. I therefore defined some test goals to achieve these objectives.

Test Goals

  • Observe the learnability of
    the app & how easily users
    can accomplish basic tasks.

  • Examine the efficiency of the user
    by measuring the amount of
    time spent on tasks.

  • To measure the amount of
    errors a user makes along
    with their severity.

Testing

Participants were recruited via Slack channels, Facebook groups, as well as my
professional & personal network. I then defined 6 essential tasks for the
participant to complete before informing them & receiving their consent.

4 tests were moderated remotely using tools such as Skype, Zoom & Google
Meet. 2 tests were moderated in-person & were completed in my own home.

My goals for round 2 of user testing were to ensure the improvements I had
made were successful & to find further problems that were missed in round 1.
